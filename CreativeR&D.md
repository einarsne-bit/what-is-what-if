## 1. What Creative mode is *for*

The method's core move is the **descriptive → hypothetical leap**: turning a *What Is?* observation into a *What If?* idea. Creative mode is the **divergent engine** that drives that leap — deliberately, playfully, and from the project's own real material.

Three commitments shape it:

- **Divergence, not judgement.** Ideation research is consistent: separate *generating* from *evaluating*. The first half of any ideation session is divergent ("going out") — volume, no criticism, build on each other — and only then convergent ("coming in"): cluster, merge, narrow ([NN/g — Diverge & Converge](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/diverge-converge/)). Creative mode owns the **divergent** half; the **gallery + analysis** (affinity, annotation, breadth) already do the convergent half. They're two ends of one arc.
- **Grounded speculation.** The What If? should spring from a real What Is? — that's the method's soul (R&D §1). So the spark always starts from the project's own observations, and a generated idea can be **saved as a What If? card pre-linked** to the observation that sparked it.
- **Low threshold for citizens.** Type-2 users (workshop participants) need one clear action, playfulness, minimal typing. The spark/shuffle interaction must be a single obvious move.

## 2. The research foundations (and how each maps to the cards)

### A. Forced connections / combinatorial creativity — *combine two observations*
Forcing a link between two unrelated items "short-circuits logical thinking and activates lateral thinking," escaping the shared comfort-zone that makes ordinary brainstorming go stale ([Toolshero](https://www.toolshero.com/creativity/forced-connections/), [Atomic Object — Design Thinking Toolkit](https://spin.atomicobject.com/design-thinking-forced-connections/)). **Combinatorial creativity** is the cognitive engine: novel ideas come from "unfamiliar combinations of familiar concepts," and *random* pairing measurably speeds ideation — the "Combinator" tool let participants reach ideas faster ([Cambridge — The Combinator](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/design-science/article/combinator-a-computerbased-tool-for-creative-idea-generation-based-on-a-simulation-approach/12C723397EB477F421699D02A025E724), [Ness Labs — Combinational creativity](https://nesslabs.com/combinational-creativity)).
**Maps to:** the *built* creative mode — two **What Is?** cards side by side; the juxtaposition is the prompt. Favouring **distant pairings** (cross-theme) is exactly what combinatorial creativity prizes (the surprising bridge), and matches the project's "cross-theme" spark mode.

### B. Constraint-based creativity — *observation + a constraint*
Counter-intuitively, **constraints raise creativity**. Studies find an **inverted-U**: too little freedom *or* too much both hurt; the "right amount" of constraint maximises novelty, partly by lifting intrinsic motivation and forcing a wider creative search ([Creativity from constraints — ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1871187122001870), [Creativity Research Journal — Limited Imagination](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/10400419.2023.2182038)). Constraints work via two functions: an **exclusionary** one ("avoid this") and a **focusing** one ("use this") ([Design Society](https://www.designsociety.org/download-publication/40712/THE+INS+AND+OUTS+OF+THE+CONSTRAINT-+CREATIVITY+RELATIONSHIP)). Constraint-based *creativity-support systems* "can significantly boost radical creativity" ([Emerald — constraint-based design principles](https://www.emerald.com/itp/article/38/8/140/1275103/Less-is-sometimes-more-constraint-based-design)).
**Maps to:** the *briefed* creative mode — one **What Is?** + a **constraint prompt** ("Make it 10× cheaper", "What if it only lasted 24 hours?", "Who is excluded from this?"). The prompt supplies a tuned, motivating constraint.

### C. Lateral thinking & provocation (de Bono) — *the prompt vocabulary*
De Bono's **random entry** (apply a random noun to the problem) and **PO / provocation** (deliberately advance an unlikely or absurd statement to escape binary logic) are the canonical engines behind a prompt deck; **Oblique Strategies** is the same idea as a card deck for creative unsticking ([Wikipedia — Lateral thinking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lateral_thinking), [IxDF — Lateral thinking](https://ixdf.org/literature/topics/lateral-thinking)). *(De Bono's empirical basis is contested — treat these as practical heuristics, not proven science.)*
**Maps to:** the **constraint-prompt library** — a mix of focusing constraints, exclusionary constraints, and pure provocations.

### D. Speculative design (Dunne & Raby) — *why "What If?" at all*
"Everything starts with a **What-if question** … to open up spaces of discussion and debate"; speculative artifacts are **provocations, not products**, and the aim is "social dreaming" about futures we do (and don't) want ([MIT Press — Speculative Everything](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262019842/speculative-everything/), [Medium — What If… Speculative Everything](https://medium.com/global-design-futures/what-if-699af21053da)). This is the intellectual home of the tool's *What If?* card.
**Maps to:** framing — Creative mode produces **provocations to discuss**, not finished proposals; encourage bold/negative/strange What Ifs, then bring them back to the workshop for annotation.

### E. Card-based ideation tools (HCI) — *form factor precedent*
150+ card-based design tools exist (IDEO Method Cards, VSD Envisioning Cards, Design with Intent); cards are valued because they're "simple, tangible, easy to manipulate," introduce novel perspectives, **maintain focus**, **stimulate discussion**, and specifically help "when ideation becomes unproductive" ([Hsieh et al., CHI 2023](https://faculty.washington.edu/garyhs/docs/hsieh-CHI2023-designcards.pdf), [Ideation decks](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/221441244_Ideation_decks_A_card-based_design_ideation_tool)).
**Maps to:** the constraint prompts should read as a **deck** — a styled, shuffleable card distinct from What Is?/What If? — validating the brief's "constraint tile" idea.

### F. Workshop facilitation — *where the spark sits in a session*
Run **silent, independent ideation first** to prevent groupthink, then share/cluster ([NN/g — Design Studio](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/facilitating-design-studio-workshop/), [Franco London — Idea-generation workshop](https://francolondon.me/my-blog/how-to-structure-an-idea-generation-workshop)). **Externalising** an idea (writing/drawing it) forces elaboration and sharing.
**Maps to:** Creative mode = the silent-divergent station; "turn this into a What If? card" is the externalisation step; the gallery/analysis is the convergence.

## 3. The §C.7 direction decision — informed by the research

The open question (built version *combines two What Is? cards* vs. brief's *What Is? + constraint prompt*) is really **two distinct, validated techniques**, not a right-vs-wrong:

| Mode | Technique | Strength |
|---|---|---|
| **Combine two What Is?** | forced connections / combinatorial creativity (§2A) | grounded in *two* real observations; surfaces cross-theme bridges |
| **What Is? + constraint prompt** | constraint-based + provocation + speculative "what if" (§2B–D) | tuned, motivating constraint; most citizen-friendly; most "What If?"-native |

**Recommendation:** treat them as **two spark modes in one view**, not an either/or — this also fits the existing `creative.html` mode switch (Random / By theme / Cross-theme). Concretely:
1. **Lead with the constraint-prompt mode** (one observation + a prompt) as the default — it's the most distinctive, the most aligned with the *What If?* framing, the lowest-threshold for citizens, and **no-AI** to build.
2. **Keep card-combination** as a second mode ("cross-pollinate two observations"), favouring distant/cross-theme pairs.
3. Both end the same way: **"Turn this into a What If?"** → pre-fills `create.html` with the source observation(s) linked and the prompt seeded into the body.

This directly serves the Analysis **breadth panel's "↪ Spark ideas in creative mode"** hooks (a theme rich in observations but thin on ideas hands off here).

## 4. Constraint-prompt library (V1, no-AI)

A plain array in `data.js` (~40–60 prompts), each tagged by family so the shuffle can balance or filter. Families drawn from the research:

- **Reverse / invert** (provocation, PO): "Do the opposite." "What if the problem were the solution?"
- **Scale** (focusing constraint): "Make it 10× cheaper." "Make it work for 10,000 people." "Make it tiny."
- **Time** (constraint): "What if it only lasted 24 hours?" "What if it had to be instant?" "What if it took a year?"
- **Subtract / exclude** (exclusionary constraint): "Remove the most important part." "What if it were free?" "Do it with no technology."
- **Who** (speculative / equity): "Who is excluded from this — design for them." "What would a child do?" "Design it for your grandmother."
- **Provoke / absurd** (de Bono PO): "What if it were illegal?" "What if it were alive?" "What if everyone could see it?"
- **Random entry** (random word): a random noun the user connects to the observation.

Design notes from the evidence:
- **Aim for the "right amount" of constraint** (inverted-U) — prompts that focus *and* leave room; avoid over-specifying.
- Offer both **focusing** and **exclusionary** prompts (the two functions).
- **Constraint tile colour:** the brief suggests **yellow/orange** — deliberately *not* green or pink, so it reads as a different *kind* of input than a What Is?/What If? card.

## 5. Interaction & visualisation patterns

- **Juxtaposition is the prompt:** two cards/tiles side by side (observation + prompt, or observation + observation). Same card aesthetic; the constraint tile is visually distinct.
- **Shuffle + lock:** one button re-rolls the pair; lock either side and re-shuffle the other (keep an observation, try new constraints — or vice versa). **Spacebar = shuffle** (low-threshold, kiosk-friendly).
- **Favour distant pairings** in combine-mode (cross-theme) — combinatorial creativity values the surprising bridge.
- **Externalise:** "Turn this into a What If?" → `create.html` pre-linked + prompt seeded. This is the one action that matters.
- **Empty / thin-project state:** if there are few What Is? cards, say so and point back to fieldwork (you can't speculate from nothing).
- **Session framing (optional):** a light "divergent mode" tone — generate freely, judge later in the gallery.
