What is this?

What is? / What if? is a tool for creating, sharing, and organising insight and idea cards in collaborative design and research processes.

It is built for design researchers and teams doing participatory and explorative work — where capturing observations and generating ideas happen side by side, and where the material needs to be shared across a team and revisited over time.

WHAT IS?

Insight cards capture observations, findings, and patterns from fieldwork, interviews, or research. They describe something you have seen, heard, or learned.

Use them to document and share what you know about a situation, a person, or a context.

WHAT IF?

Idea cards capture speculative ideas, provocations, and design directions. They describe something that could be — a possible future, an intervention, or an alternative way of doing things.

Use them to explore and communicate possibilities, linked to the insights that inspired them.

How to use it

  • Cards belong to a project. Each project has its own collection of What is? and What if? cards.
  • Create cards with + New what is or + New what if. Write directly on the card, add tags, a name, and optionally an image.
  • The gallery shows both card types together. Use the type filter (All / What is? / What if?) and the filter bar to sort, search, or narrow by author and theme.
  • Open the Creative view to spark new What if? ideas from your What is? cards, and the Analysis view to see patterns across the project.
  • Export cards to a PDF — one card per A4 page — for workshops, pin-up sessions, and printing.
